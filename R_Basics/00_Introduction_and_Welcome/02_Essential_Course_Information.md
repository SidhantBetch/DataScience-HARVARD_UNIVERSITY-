## 🎡 **Course Syllabus**
The course Syllabus and FAQs (see link at top of page) contains a more comprehensive version of the information below - but if you don't read the syllabus, please read this!

## 🐦‍🔥 **Course Structure**
This is a self-paced course. You can work on it with your own timeline in mind. However, we suggest that you work on a section per week, and it will take you 2 to 4 hours per week.

Check the course home page for important dates. If you are interested in pursuing a Verified Certificate, you will need to upgrade before the date listed on the home page and finish all assignments before course close.

## 🚐 **Assignments**
Some of the assignments in the course provide opportunities to practice coding in R. R is a flexible programming language designed partly for readability and ease of programming.

**Ed Exercises** allow you to practice your coding skills using the Ed platform. For these exercises, the Ed platform will give you a set of code to modify and run in your browser. You can request hints within the Ed platform. Remember that you can also go to the Discussion Board External link for help. You can take as many attempts as you need. When your code works correctly, you will be awarded points to track your progress. These are for your reference only; points from the Ed exercises do not count toward your grade or the certificate.

Some Quiz Questions are based on coding you do on your own. To answer these questions, you will need to install R. You may also want to install RStudio. Installation instructions can be found within the course.

## ⚓ **Passing Rate**
The passing rate is 70%. You must sign up for a Verified Certificate and earn a grade of 70% or higher in order to obtain a certificate for this course.

## 🎡 **Note on Accessibility**
Screenshots are used throughout the course to show what your screen might look like during a programming exercise. These screenshots are described in the video and/or in the text of the platform. 

All the important code in the videos will also be provided as readable text (transcript) on the platform.

Occasionally in the videos, the instructor may say something like "as you see here" to refer to an image, code, or URL. That information will also be provided as screenreader-accessible text.

All images are captioned or have alternative text descriptions associated with them.
